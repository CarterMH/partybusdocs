Standard Operating Procedures (SOP)

Document Number: SOP-PBVA-001
Revision: 1.2
Effective Date: March 1, 2008
Supersedes: SOP-PBVA-001 Rev 1.1

This document establishes standard operating procedures for all PartyBus Virtual Airline flight operations. All pilots must comply with these procedures. Deviation from SOPs is only permitted in emergency situations or when explicitly authorized by operations management.

Mandatory Compliance: All SOPs are mandatory. Failure to comply may result in disciplinary action.
1. General Procedures

1.1 Crew Resource Management

All pilots operating PartyBus VA aircraft must follow crew resource management principles:

  • Maintain situational awareness at all times
  • Communicate clearly and effectively
  • Use all available resources (checklists, charts, manuals)
  • Verify critical actions independently
  • Question unsafe actions or procedures
  • Maintain professional demeanor

1.2 Standard Phraseology

Standard aviation phraseology must be used in all radio communications:

  • Use standard ICAO phraseology
  • Speak clearly and at appropriate volume
  • Use phonetic alphabet for call signs and critical information
  • Avoid non-standard terminology
  • Read back all clearances, instructions, and frequency changes

1.3 Documentation Requirements

The following documentation must be available for all flights:

  • Current charts for departure and arrival airports
  • En-route charts for the flight path
  • Aircraft operating manual (POH)
  • PartyBus VA checklists
  • Emergency procedures reference
  • ACARS operating manual
2. Pre-Flight Procedures

2.1 Flight Planning

Flight planning must be completed before every flight:

  1. Select route from approved route database
  2. Review route details (distance, estimated flight time, airports)
  3. Check weather conditions at departure, en-route, and arrival
  4. Calculate fuel requirements (trip fuel + reserves + alternate)
  5. Determine required runway lengths and verify adequacy
  6. Review NOTAMs for relevant airports
  7. Check aircraft availability and configuration
  8. Complete weight and balance calculations (if required)
  9. File flight plan in ACARS system

2.2 Aircraft Inspection

Complete pre-flight inspection per checklist:

  • Exterior walkaround inspection (see checklists)
  • Verify fuel quantity meets requirements
  • Check all control surfaces for freedom of movement
  • Inspect tires, struts, and landing gear
  • Verify all lights are functional
  • Check for fluid leaks or visible damage

2.3 Cockpit Preparation

Cockpit preparation must include:

  • All switches in proper position for startup
  • Flight plan loaded in navigation system
  • Radio frequencies programmed
  • ACARS system started and connected
  • Checklists available and accessible
  • Charts readily available
3. Engine Start and Taxi Procedures

3.1 Engine Start

Engine start must follow approved checklist:

  • Ensure parking brake is set
  • Verify area is clear before starting engines
  • Start engines in sequence specified in checklist
  • Monitor engine instruments during start
  • Verify oil pressure rises within 30 seconds
  • Check for abnormal sounds or vibrations
  • Verify all engine parameters in normal range

3.2 Taxi Procedures

Taxi operations must comply with:

  • Obtain ground control clearance before moving
  • Taxi at safe speed (maximum 20 knots, 10 knots in turns)
  • Maintain awareness of other aircraft and vehicles
  • Follow taxiway centerline
  • Yield right-of-way to aircraft on active runway
  • Use taxi lights during taxi
  • Complete before takeoff checklist during taxi
4. Takeoff Procedures

4.1 Pre-Takeoff

Before takeoff, verify:

  • Runway is correct and clear
  • Wind direction and velocity noted
  • All checklist items completed
  • Takeoff clearance obtained
  • Aircraft properly configured (flaps, trim)
  • Navigation systems set and verified

4.2 Takeoff Execution

Takeoff must be performed as follows:

  • Line up on runway centerline
  • Apply takeoff power smoothly and continuously
  • Maintain centerline during takeoff roll
  • Rotate at published Vr (rotation speed)
  • Establish positive rate of climb before retracting gear
  • Follow departure procedure or ATC instructions
  • Maintain appropriate climb speed

4.3 Standard Takeoff Speeds (Typical)

Speed Designation Purpose
V1 Decision Speed Maximum speed to abort takeoff
Vr Rotation Speed Speed to begin rotation
V2 Takeoff Safety Speed Minimum climb speed after takeoff
5. Climb Procedures

5.1 Initial Climb

After takeoff:

  • Maintain V2 until safe altitude (typically 400 feet AGL)
  • Retract landing gear after positive rate confirmed
  • Retract flaps on schedule (as specified in checklist)
  • Accelerate to climb speed
  • Contact departure control as required
  • Follow departure procedure or ATC vectors

5.2 En-Route Climb

During climb to cruise altitude:

  • Maintain recommended climb speed (typically 250 knots below 10,000 feet)
  • Comply with altitude restrictions
  • Monitor engine parameters
  • Respond to ATC altitude assignments
  • Maintain awareness of terrain and obstacles
6. Cruise Procedures

6.1 Cruise Configuration

Upon reaching cruise altitude:

  • Set cruise power
  • Establish cruise speed (typically Mach 0.78)
  • Verify navigation system tracking correctly
  • Monitor fuel consumption
  • Verify ACARS tracking flight data

6.2 Cruise Monitoring

During cruise flight:

  • Monitor engine instruments continuously
  • Verify navigation system operation
  • Monitor fuel quantity and consumption
  • Maintain assigned altitude (+/- 100 feet)
  • Monitor weather conditions
  • Maintain radio communication as required
  • Review arrival procedures and charts

6.3 Altitude Deviation Limits

  • Maintain assigned altitude within +/- 100 feet
  • Report altitude deviations greater than 200 feet to ATC
  • Execute altitude changes smoothly (500-1000 fpm typical)
7. Descent Procedures

7.1 Descent Planning

Before beginning descent:

  • Calculate top of descent point (typically 3 nm per 1000 feet altitude)
  • Review arrival procedure and charts
  • Obtain current altimeter setting for destination
  • Brief arrival and approach procedures
  • Verify fuel quantity adequate
  • Contact approach control

7.2 Descent Execution

During descent:

  • Reduce power smoothly
  • Maintain appropriate descent rate (1500-2500 fpm typical)
  • Monitor airspeed (do not exceed Vmo/Mmo)
  • Comply with altitude restrictions
  • Set altimeters to local QNH
  • Follow ATC instructions or published procedure
8. Approach Procedures

8.1 Approach Briefing

Approach briefing must include:

  • Type of approach (ILS, VOR, RNAV, visual)
  • Runway and approach identifier
  • Minimum descent altitude or decision height
  • Missed approach procedure
  • Landing runway length and condition
  • Wind direction and velocity
  • Required aircraft configuration

8.2 Approach Configuration

Configure aircraft for approach:

  • Extend landing gear at appropriate point
  • Set flaps progressively as specified
  • Set trim for approach
  • Establish approach speed (typically Vref + 5-10 knots)
  • Verify landing checklist completed

8.3 Final Approach

On final approach:

  • Maintain proper glidepath (typically 3 degrees)
  • Keep aircraft aligned with runway centerline
  • Maintain approach speed
  • Monitor descent rate
  • Be prepared for go-around if necessary
9. Landing Procedures

9.1 Landing Technique

Standard landing procedure:

  • Maintain approach speed until flare
  • Begin flare at approximately 30-50 feet above runway
  • Reduce power smoothly during flare
  • Touch down on main landing gear first
  • Maintain nose-up attitude until nose gear touches
  • Land within first 3000 feet of runway
  • Maintain centerline (within 20 feet)

9.2 Landing Performance Standards

  • Landing rate: Not to exceed -600 fpm
  • Touchdown point: Within first 3000 feet of runway
  • Centerline deviation: Not to exceed 20 feet
  • Airspeed at touchdown: Within 5 knots of target

9.3 After Landing

Immediately after landing:

  • Apply reverse thrust if available
  • Use wheel brakes as needed
  • Maintain centerline during rollout
  • Retract speed brakes
  • Disengage reverse thrust at appropriate speed
  • Retract flaps
  • Turn off landing lights
  • Contact ground control when clear of runway
10. Go-Around Procedures

10.1 Go-Around Decision

Go-around must be executed if:

  • Aircraft is not stabilized by 500 feet AGL
  • Runway is not clear
  • Landing is not assured
  • ATC issues go-around instruction
  • Equipment malfunction occurs

10.2 Go-Around Execution

Standard go-around procedure:

  1. Announce "Go-around" clearly
  2. Apply takeoff/go-around power immediately
  3. Rotate to climb attitude
  4. Retract flaps incrementally (to takeoff position)
  5. Retract landing gear after positive rate confirmed
  6. Climb to assigned altitude or follow missed approach procedure
  7. Contact ATC to advise of go-around
  8. Follow ATC instructions for subsequent approach
11. Post-Flight Procedures

11.1 Taxi to Gate

  • Follow ground control taxi instructions
  • Taxi at safe speed
  • Complete after landing checklist
  • Maintain awareness of other traffic

11.2 Shutdown

Engine shutdown must follow checklist:

  • Set parking brake
  • Allow engines to idle for cooling (1-2 minutes)
  • Shut down engines per checklist
  • Turn off all systems
  • Secure aircraft

11.3 Flight Report

  • Complete ACARS flight log
  • Verify all flight data is recorded
  • Submit flight report through ACARS
  • Review flight performance data
  • Note any discrepancies or issues
12. Special Procedures

12.1 Reduced Visibility Operations

When operating in reduced visibility:

  • Follow instrument approach procedures
  • Use all available navigation aids
  • Maintain strict adherence to published procedures
  • Execute missed approach if visual contact not established

12.2 High Density Traffic Operations

  • Maintain increased situational awareness
  • Follow ATC instructions precisely
  • Monitor radio frequency closely
  • Be prepared for delays and holding

12.3 Mountain Operations

  • Consider terrain clearance carefully
  • Maintain appropriate altitude above terrain
  • Account for density altitude effects
  • Review terrain clearance procedures
13. Record Keeping

13.1 Flight Logging

All flights must be logged via ACARS:

  • Flight duration (block to block)
  • Distance flown
  • Fuel consumed
  • Landing performance
  • Any discrepancies or incidents

13.2 Incident Reporting

The following must be reported:

  • Deviations from procedures
  • Equipment malfunctions
  • ATC communication issues
  • Unsafe situations
  • Rule violations (self-reported)
