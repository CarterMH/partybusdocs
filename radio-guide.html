Radio Communications Guide

Proper radio communication is essential when flying online with ATC networks like VATSIM or IVAO.

Basic Communication Format

Standard format: Who you're calling - Who you are - What you want

Common Phrases
Initial Contact:
"Seattle Center, PartyBus 123, request clearance"

Readback:
"PartyBus 123, cleared as filed"

Request:
"PartyBus 123, request climb to flight level 350"

Handoff:
"PartyBus 123, contact Los Angeles Center on 135.25"
Phonetic Alphabet

Use the phonetic alphabet for your callsign and important information:

A - Alpha B - Bravo C - Charlie
D - Delta E - Echo F - Foxtrot
Frequency Ranges
  • Ground Control: 121.6 - 121.9 MHz
  • Tower: 118.0 - 121.6 MHz
  • Departure/Approach: 119.0 - 128.0 MHz
  • Center: 128.0 - 135.0 MHz
Tip: Practice radio communications before flying online. Listen to live ATC to learn proper phraseology.
