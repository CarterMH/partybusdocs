Pilot Operating Handbook (POH)

Airplane Type: Airbus A319/A320/A321 (CEO Variant)
Manual Number: POH-PBVA-A320-001
Revision: 2.1
Date: March 1, 2008
Applicable to: All PartyBus VA A319/A320/A321 CEO aircraft

This Pilot Operating Handbook contains information required for the safe and efficient operation of the Airbus A320 family aircraft in PartyBus VA service. This manual supplements but does not replace the aircraft manufacturer's documentation.

Limitation: This handbook is for virtual flight simulation purposes only. Actual aircraft operations require manufacturer-approved documentation and proper certification.

Section 1: General Information

1.1 Introduction

The Airbus A320 family consists of three variants operated by PartyBus VA: A319, A320, and A321, all in the CEO (Current Engine Option) configuration. These aircraft share common type ratings and similar operating characteristics, with differences primarily in capacity and range.

1.2 Aircraft Identification

A320 A320 320 EASA.IM.A.174
A321 A321 321 EASA.IM.A.174

A320 Specifications:

  • Wingspan: 34.10 m (111.9 ft)
  • Height: 11.76 m (38.6 ft)
  • Wheelbase: 12.64 m (41.5 ft)
  • Wing area: 122.6 m² (1,320 sq ft)
  • Aspect ratio: 9.5:1
  • Wing sweep: 25 degrees
  • Maximum fuselage width: 3.95 m (13.0 ft)

A321 Specifications:

  • Wingspan: 34.10 m (111.9 ft)
  • Height: 11.76 m (38.6 ft)
  • Wheelbase: 18.60 m (61.0 ft)
  • Wing area: 122.6 m² (1,320 sq ft)
  • Aspect ratio: 9.5:1
  • Wing sweep: 25 degrees
  • Maximum fuselage width: 3.95 m (13.0 ft)
  • Cabin width: 3.70 m (12.1 ft)

1.4 Powerplant

All PartyBus VA A320 family aircraft are equipped with either:

  • CFM International CFM56-5B series engines, or
  • International Aero Engines (IAE) V2500 series engines

CFM56-5B Engine Specifications:

  • Thrust range: 22,000 - 27,000 lbf (98 - 120 kN)
  • Bypass ratio: 6.0:1
  • Pressure ratio: 32.8:1
  • Fan diameter: 1.76 m (69.0 in)

IAE V2500 Engine Specifications:

  • Thrust range: 22,000 - 33,000 lbf (98 - 147 kN)
  • Bypass ratio: 4.7:1 to 5.4:1
  • Pressure ratio: 34.5:1
  • Fan diameter: 1.60 m (63.0 in)

2.2 Weight Limitations

A319:

  • Maximum Takeoff Weight (MTOW): 75,500 kg (166,450 lbs)
  • Maximum Landing Weight (MLW): 66,000 kg (145,500 lbs)
  • Maximum Zero Fuel Weight (MZFW): 62,500 kg (137,800 lbs)
  • Operating Empty Weight (OEW): 40,800 kg (89,950 lbs) typical

A320:

  • Maximum Takeoff Weight (MTOW): 78,000 kg (171,960 lbs)
  • Maximum Landing Weight (MLW): 66,000 kg (145,500 lbs)
  • Maximum Zero Fuel Weight (MZFW): 64,500 kg (142,200 lbs)
  • Operating Empty Weight (OEW): 42,400 kg (93,500 lbs) typical

A321:

  • Maximum Takeoff Weight (MTOW): 93,500 kg (206,130 lbs)
  • Maximum Landing Weight (MLW): 77,800 kg (171,520 lbs)
  • Maximum Zero Fuel Weight (MZFW): 75,500 kg (166,450 lbs)
  • Operating Empty Weight (OEW): 48,500 kg (106,900 lbs) typical

2.3 Fuel Capacity

  • A319: 24,210 L (6,396 US gal) usable fuel
  • A320: 24,210 L (6,396 US gal) usable fuel
  • A321: 32,940 L (8,703 US gal) usable fuel

2.4 Center of Gravity Limits

CG must remain within the following limits:

  • Forward limit: 18% MAC (Mean Aerodynamic Chord)
  • Aft limit: 40% MAC
  • CG range: 22% MAC (typical operating range)

2.5 Altitude Limitations

  • Maximum operating altitude: 39,000 feet (FL390)
  • Certified ceiling: 39,000 feet
  • Emergency descent altitude: As required for terrain clearance

2.6 Temperature Limitations

  • Maximum operating temperature: ISA + 45°C
  • Minimum operating temperature: -54°C
  • Maximum ground temperature: 50°C

2.7 Crosswind Limitations

  • Maximum demonstrated crosswind: 38 knots
  • Recommended maximum: 30 knots
  • Wet runway reduction: Apply 10 knot reduction
Section 3: Performance

3.1 Takeoff Performance

Takeoff performance varies significantly with weight, altitude, temperature, and runway conditions. Use performance charts for specific calculations.

Typical Takeoff Distances (A320, ISA, Sea Level, MTOW):

  • Takeoff distance: 2,090 m (6,860 ft)
  • Accelerate-stop distance: 2,090 m (6,860 ft)
  • Takeoff field length: 2,090 m (6,860 ft)

3.2 Landing Performance

Typical Landing Distances (A320, MLW, Dry Runway):

  • Landing distance required: 1,530 m (5,020 ft)
  • Wet runway increase: +15% typically
  • Runway slope effects: Consult performance charts

3.3 Climb Performance

Typical Climb Gradients:

  • One engine inoperative: 2.4% minimum
  • All engines operating: Varies by weight and altitude
  • Typical climb rate: 1,500 - 3,000 fpm

3.4 Cruise Performance

Typical Cruise Speeds:

  • Long range cruise: Mach 0.78 (448 knots)
  • Maximum cruise: Mach 0.82 (470 knots)
  • Optimal altitude: FL310 - FL390 depending on weight

3.5 Fuel Consumption

Typical Fuel Flow (A320, Cruise, FL350, Mach 0.78):

  • Per engine: 2,200 - 2,500 kg/h (4,850 - 5,510 lbs/h)
  • Total: 4,400 - 5,000 kg/h (9,700 - 11,020 lbs/h)
  • Specific range: 0.046 - 0.052 nm/kg (varies by conditions)

3.6 Range Performance

  • A319: 6,850 km (3,700 nm) typical range
  • A320: 6,100 km (3,300 nm) typical range
  • A321: 5,950 km (3,200 nm) typical range
Section 4: Aircraft Systems

4.1 Flight Control System

The A320 family uses fly-by-wire flight control system:

  • Primary flight controls: Elevators, ailerons, rudder
  • Secondary flight controls: Flaps, slats, spoilers, trim
  • Control laws: Normal law, alternate law, direct law
  • Protection modes: High angle of attack, high speed, bank angle

4.2 Hydraulic System

  • Number of systems: 3 independent systems
  • System pressure: 3,000 psi (207 bar)
  • Green system: Primary flight controls, landing gear, brakes
  • Blue system: Standby systems, brakes
  • Yellow system: Primary flight controls, landing gear, brakes

4.3 Electrical System

  • Primary: Engine-driven generators (115V AC, 400 Hz)
  • Secondary: APU generator (115V AC, 400 Hz)
  • Emergency: Battery and static inverter
  • Voltage: 115V AC, 28V DC

4.4 Fuel System

  • Fuel tanks: 2 wing tanks (integral)
  • Fuel capacity: As specified in Section 2.3
  • Fuel pumps: Engine-driven and electric boost pumps
  • Fuel transfer: Automatic crossfeed capability

4.5 Pressurization System

  • Cabin altitude: Maximum 8,000 feet at cruise altitude
  • Rate of change: Controlled, maximum 500 fpm climb, 350 fpm descent
  • Differential pressure: Maximum 8.6 psi

4.6 Landing Gear System

  • Configuration: Tricycle, retractable
  • Gear retraction: Hydraulic, time: 6-8 seconds
  • Brake system: Multiple disc, anti-skid equipped
  • Nose wheel steering: Hydraulic, +/- 75 degrees
Section 5: Normal Procedures

5.1 Engine Start Procedures

Refer to approved checklists for detailed procedures. General sequence:

  1. Verify parking brake set
  2. Fuel pumps ON
  3. Start switch ON
  4. Monitor engine parameters
  5. Verify oil pressure rises within 30 seconds
  6. Verify stable idle RPM

5.2 Taxi Procedures

  • Maximum taxi speed: 20 knots straight, 10 knots in turns
  • Use rudder pedals for directional control
  • Monitor brake temperature during extended taxi
  • Follow ground control instructions

5.3 Takeoff Procedures

  • Flaps typically set to Flaps 1+F for takeoff
  • Apply takeoff power smoothly
  • Rotate at Vr speed
  • Maintain V2 until safe altitude
  • Retract gear after positive rate confirmed

5.4 Cruise Procedures

  • Maintain assigned altitude within +/- 100 feet
  • Monitor engine parameters continuously
  • Adjust power as needed for speed control
  • Monitor fuel consumption

5.5 Approach and Landing Procedures

  • Extend landing gear by 10,000 feet AGL
  • Set flaps progressively: Flaps 1, Flaps 2, Flaps 3, Full
  • Maintain approach speed: Vref + 5-10 knots
  • Land on main gear first
  • Use reverse thrust and brakes for deceleration
Section 6: Weight and Balance

6.1 Weight Definitions

  • Operating Empty Weight (OEW): Aircraft weight with standard equipment, no fuel, no payload
  • Zero Fuel Weight (ZFW): OEW + payload only
  • Takeoff Weight (TOW): ZFW + fuel
  • Landing Weight: TOW - fuel consumed

6.2 Balance Calculations

CG position must be calculated before each flight using weight and balance charts. Ensure CG remains within limits throughout flight as fuel is consumed.

6.3 Loading

  • Distribute load evenly when possible
  • Secure all cargo and baggage
  • Verify weight does not exceed limits
  • Calculate CG position
Section 7: Emergency Procedures

Emergency procedures are detailed in the Emergency Procedures manual. This section provides quick reference for critical emergencies.

7.1 Engine Fire

  1. Engine fire warning - CONFIRM
  2. Engine fire switch - PULL (discharges extinguisher)
  3. Throttle - IDLE
  4. Shut down engine per checklist
  5. Land at nearest suitable airport

7.2 Engine Failure

  1. Maintain aircraft control
  2. Establish best glide or climb speed
  3. Secure failed engine
  4. Declare emergency
  5. Proceed to nearest suitable airport

7.3 Loss of Pressurization

  1. Descend immediately to 10,000 feet or below
  2. Don oxygen masks
  3. Emergency descent procedure
  4. Land at nearest suitable airport
Note: For complete emergency procedures, refer to the Emergency Procedures manual. This handbook provides basic guidance only.
Section 8: Handling, Service, and Maintenance

8.1 Pre-Flight Inspection

Complete walkaround inspection per checklist. Verify:

  • Exterior condition - no visible damage
  • Control surfaces - free and correct movement
  • Landing gear - tires, struts, doors
  • Engines - cowlings, intake, exhaust
  • Lights - all functional
  • Fuel - quantity verified

8.2 Post-Flight Inspection

  • Visual inspection for leaks or damage
  • Secure aircraft
  • Complete flight log
  • Report any discrepancies
